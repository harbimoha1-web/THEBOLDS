```
FORMING → STORMING → NORMING → PERFORMING → ADJOURNING
   ↓         ↓          ↓          ↓            ↓

```

## Lencioni's Five Dysfunctions of a Team

```
      ┌─────────────────────────────┐
      │  Inattention to RESULTS     │ ← Focus on personal goals
      ├─────────────────────────────┤
      │  Avoidance of ACCOUNTABILITY│ ← Low standards
      ├─────────────────────────────┤
      │  Lack of COMMITMENT         │ ← Ambiguity
      ├─────────────────────────────┤
      │  Fear of CONFLICT           │ ← Artificial harmony
      ├─────────────────────────────┤
      │  Absence of TRUST           │ ← Invulnerability
      └─────────────────────────────┘
              ▲ Foundation
```

### 1. Absence of Trust (Foundation)
**Symptoms:**
- Hide weaknesses and mistakes
- Hesitate to ask for help
- Jump to conclusions about intentions
- Dread meetings

**Solutions:**
- Leader models vulnerability first
- Personal history exercise
- Acknowledge mistakes openly
- Behavioral profiling (understand each other)

### 2. Fear of Conflict
**Symptoms:**
- Boring meetings
- Back-channel discussions
- Topics avoided
- Passive-aggressive behavior

**Solutions:**
- Mine for conflict intentionally
- Give permission to disagree
- Real-time permission ("It's okay to push back")
- Conflict norms established

### 3. Lack of Commitment
**Symptoms:**
- Ambiguity about direction
- Revisiting decisions
- Second-guessing
- Analysis paralysis

**Solutions:**
- Cascading messaging (clarity from top)
- Deadlines for decisions
- Disagree and commit culture
- Worst-case scenario planning

### 4. Avoidance of Accountability
**Symptoms:**
- Low standards accepted
- Deadlines missed
- Leader as sole source of discipline
- Resentment of high performers

**Solutions:**
- Publication of goals and standards
- Simple progress reviews
- Team rewards (collective accountability)
- Peer pressure (positive)

### 5. Inattention to Results
**Symptoms:**
- Status and ego prioritized
- Team loses often
- Team stagnates
- Achievement-oriented members leave

**Solutions:**
- Public results tracking
- Results-based rewards
- Leader models results focus
- Minimize individual recognition

## Psychological Safety (Amy Edmondson)

### What Is Psychological Safety?
**"A shared belief that the team is safe for interpersonal risk-taking."**

Members feel comfortable:
- Asking questions
- Admitting mistakes
- Proposing new ideas
- Disagreeing with others
- Taking risks

### The Four Stages of Psychological Safety

```
STAGE 1: Inclusion Safety
└── Feeling accepted and belonging

STAGE 2: Learner Safety
└── Feeling safe to ask questions and make mistakes

STAGE 3: Contributor Safety
└── Feeling safe to add value and participate

STAGE 4: Challenger Safety
└── Feeling safe to challenge status quo
```

### Building Psychological Safety

**Leader Behaviors:**
1. Frame work as a learning problem, not execution problem
2. Acknowledge your own fallibility
3. Model curiosity - ask lots of questions
4. Create structures for input
5. Embrace messengers (don't shoot them)
6. Destigmatize failure

**Team Practices:**
1. No interrupting
2. Equal speaking time
3. Celebrate "good failures"
4. Start with appreciation
5. "What can we learn?" not "Who's to blame?"
6. Private criticism, public praise

### Measuring Psychological Safety

Ask team members (anonymous):
1. If I make a mistake, it's held against me
2. Team members can bring up problems and tough issues
3. People are sometimes rejected for being different
4. It's safe to take a risk on this team
5. It's difficult to ask others for help
6. No one would deliberately undermine my efforts
7. My unique skills are valued and utilized

## Radical Candor (Kim Scott)

```
                    CARE PERSONALLY
                          ↑
                          │
       RUINOUS       ┌────┼────┐    RADICAL
       EMPATHY       │    │    │    CANDOR
       (nice but     │    │    │    (the goal)
        unhelpful)   │    │    │
    ←─────────────── │────┼────│ ──────────────→
                     │    │    │    CHALLENGE
        OBNOXIOUS    │    │    │    DIRECTLY
        AGGRESSION   │    │    │
        (brutal)     └────┼────┘
                          │
                          │
       MANIPULATIVE       │
       INSINCERITY        ↓
       (worst)
```

### The Four Quadrants

**Radical Candor (Care + Challenge)**
- Give honest feedback because you care
- Be direct AND kind
- "I need to tell you something because I want you to succeed..."

**Ruinous Empathy (Care, No Challenge)**
- Avoiding hard truths to be "nice"
- Leads to blindsided failures
- "Everything's fine" when it's not

**Obnoxious Aggression (Challenge, No Care)**
- Brutal honesty without humanity
- Creates fear and resentment
- "This is terrible, do better"

**Manipulative Insincerity (No Care, No Challenge)**
- Saying what people want to hear
- Backstabbing, politics
- "Great job!" (then complains to others)

### Giving Radical Candor Feedback

**Framework: HIP (Humble, Helpful, Immediate, Private/Public)**
- **Humble:** You might be wrong
- **Helpful:** Focus on helping them, not venting
- **Immediate:** Don't wait
- **Private:** Criticism in private, praise in public

**Formula:**
1. Show you care (genuine)
2. State the observation (specific, not judgment)
3. Explain the impact
4. Invite dialogue
5. Suggest path forward

**Example:**
"Hey, I noticed in the meeting you interrupted Bee twice. I don't think you meant to, but it made her retreat and we lost her ideas. I know you value her input - maybe let her finish before responding? What do you think?"

# CONFLICT RESOLUTION

## Sources of Team Conflict

| Source | Example | Resolution Approach |
|--------|---------|---------------------|
| **Priorities** | Speed vs. quality | Clarify shared goals |
| **Resources** | Time, budget, attention | Transparent allocation |
| **Methods** | Different approaches | Test and learn |
| **Values** | Ethical boundaries | Align on principles |
| **Personality** | Working styles clash | Understanding + accommodation |
| **Communication** | Misunderstanding | Clarify and translate |
| **Competition** | Status, recognition | Reframe as collaboration |

## The Thomas-Kilmann Conflict Modes

```
                    ASSERTIVE
                        ↑
                        │
        COMPETING       │       COLLABORATING
        (Win-Lose)      │       (Win-Win)
                        │
  ←─────────────────────┼─────────────────────→
                        │                    COOPERATIVE
        AVOIDING        │       ACCOMMODATING
        (Lose-Lose)     │       (Lose-Win)
                        │
                    COMPROMISING
                     (Split)
                        ↓
```

### When to Use Each Mode

| Mode | Use When |
|------|----------|
| **Competing** | Emergency, values at stake, unpopular decision needed |
| **Collaborating** | Important issues need creative solutions, time available |
| **Compromising** | Moderate importance, temporary solution, time pressure |
| **Avoiding** | Issue trivial, need cooling off, others can resolve better |
| **Accommodating** | You're wrong, issue more important to other party, goodwill |

## Conflict Resolution Process

### Step 1: Acknowledge
- Name the conflict
- Get agreement to resolve it
- Create safe space

### Step 2: Understand Both Sides
- Each person states their perspective
- No interruptions
- Active listening
- Reflect back what you heard

### Step 3: Find Common Ground
- What do we agree on?
- What are shared goals?
- What do we both want?

### Step 4: Identify the Real Issue
- Often different from stated issue
- Dig deeper with "why" questions
- Surface underlying needs

### Step 5: Generate Options
- Brainstorm solutions together
- No judgment during brainstorming
- Look for creative alternatives

### Step 6: Agree on Action
- Specific next steps
- Who does what by when
- How to check in
- What if it doesn't work

### Step 7: Follow Up
- Check on implementation
- Celebrate progress
- Address new issues quickly

## Nonviolent Communication (NVC)

### The Four Components

```
OBSERVATION → FEELING → NEED → REQUEST
(What happened) (How I feel) (Why I feel it) (What I want)
```

**Template:**
"When I [observation], I feel [feeling] because I need [need]. Would you be willing to [request]?"

### Examples

**Instead of:** "You never listen to my design feedback!"

**NVC:** "When I share design suggestions and don't hear a response (observation), I feel frustrated and unheard (feeling) because I need to know my input is valued (need). Would you be willing to acknowledge my suggestions, even if you disagree? (request)"

### The Four Steps Detailed

**1. Observation (Facts, not judgment)**
- Bad: "You're always late"
- Good: "In the last three meetings, you arrived 10-15 minutes after start time"

**2. Feeling (Emotion, not thought)**
- Bad: "I feel like you don't care" (that's a thought)
- Good: "I feel anxious and frustrated"

**3. Need (Universal human need)**
- Connection, autonomy, respect, efficiency, fairness, etc.
- "Because I need reliability and predictability"

**4. Request (Specific, actionable, optional)**
- Not a demand (they can say no)
- "Would you be willing to text me if you'll be late?"

# COMMUNICATION FRAMEWORKS

## The SBI Model (Situation-Behavior-Impact)

For giving specific, actionable feedback:

**Situation:** When and where
**Behavior:** What specifically was done
**Impact:** Effect on you, team, or work

**Example:**
"In yesterday's code review (situation), you rewrote my function without discussing it first (behavior). That made me feel bypassed and I worry about learning opportunities lost (impact)."

## Active Listening Techniques

| Technique | How | Purpose |
|-----------|-----|---------|
| **Paraphrasing** | "So what you're saying is..." | Confirm understanding |
| **Reflecting feelings** | "It sounds like you're frustrated" | Show empathy |
| **Clarifying questions** | "Can you give me an example?" | Get specifics |
| **Summarizing** | "Let me make sure I've got it all..." | Check completeness |
| **Silence** | Pause and wait | Create space for more |
| **Body language** | Eye contact, nodding | Show attention |

## Email/Async Communication Rules

### For the Team

1. **Lead with the ask** - Don't bury requests
2. **One topic per message** - Makes response easier
3. **Clear subject lines** - "Decision needed: X by Friday"
4. **State the expected response** - FYI, Action, Decision
5. **Deadline if needed** - Be explicit
6. **Consider synchronous** - Some things need a call

### Response Time Norms

| Channel | Expected Response |
|---------|-------------------|
| Emergency | Immediate (define what qualifies) |
| Direct message | Within hours |
| Email | Within 24 hours |
| FYI | No response needed (acknowledge if major) |

**Rules:**

- Clear problem statement
- Silent ideation first
- Share round-robin
- Group and prioritize

**Rules:**
- No criticism during generation
- Build on others' ideas
- Quantity over quality
- Wild ideas welcome

### Decision Meeting (30-60 min)
**Format:**
- Context and options (pre-read)
- Questions for clarification
- Discussion
- Decision (who decides is clear)
- Next steps

**Rules:**
- Come prepared
- Disagree and commit
- Document the decision

### Retrospective (60 min)
**Format:**
- What went well
- What didn't go well
- What will we try differently

**Rules:**
- No blame
- Focus on process not people
- Concrete actions

### 1:1 Meeting (30-60 min)
**Format:**
- Their agenda first
- Check on blockers
- Career/growth conversation
- Feedback (both directions)

**Rules:**
- Never skip
- They own the agenda
- Confidential

## Meeting Ground Rules

1. **Start on time** - Don't wait for stragglers
2. **Have an agenda** - Circulated in advance
3. **Designate roles** - Facilitator, note-taker, timekeeper
4. **Stay on topic** - Parking lot for tangents
5. **Equal participation** - Draw out quiet voices
6. **End with actions** - Who does what by when
7. **End on time** - Respect calendars

## Remote Meeting Additions

1. **Cameras on** - For important discussions
2. **Chat for questions** - Don't interrupt
3. **Explicit turn-taking** - "Bee, what do you think?"
4. **Recording option** - For those who miss it
5. **More breaks** - Zoom fatigue is real
6. **Async alternative** - Does this need to be live?
