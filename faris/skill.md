## 1.1 Stakeholder Interviews

### Structured Interviews
Pre-defined questions, consistent across all stakeholders.

**When to use:**
- Comparing perspectives across stakeholders
- Regulatory/compliance requirements
- Large stakeholder groups

**Template:**
```
INTERVIEW SCRIPT

Participant: [Name, Role]
Date: [Date]
Duration: 45-60 minutes

OPENING (5 min)
- Thank for time
- Explain purpose
- Confirm recording consent

CONTEXT (10 min)
1. What is your role in relation to this project?
2. What does success look like for you?
3. Who else should I speak with?

CURRENT STATE (15 min)
4. Walk me through how you currently [process/task]
5. What works well today?
6. What frustrates you most?

FUTURE STATE (15 min)
7. If you had a magic wand, what would change?
8. What constraints must we work within?
9. What would make you resist this change?

PRIORITIZATION (10 min)
10. Of everything we discussed, what's most critical?
11. What could we defer to a later phase?

CLOSING (5 min)
- Summarize key points
- Next steps
- Any questions?
```

### Semi-Structured Interviews
Core questions with flexibility to explore emerging topics.

**When to use:**
- Discovery phase
- Complex domains
- When you need to follow interesting threads

**The Mom Test Questions (Rob Fitzpatrick):**
| Bad Question | Good Question |
|--------------|---------------|
| "Would you use this?" | "How do you currently handle this?" |
| "Do you think it's a good idea?" | "What have you tried before?" |
| "Would you pay for this?" | "What did you spend on X last year?" |
| "What would your ideal product do?" | "Walk me through the last time you did X" |

### Key Interview Principles
1. **Talk about their life, not your idea**
2. **Ask about specifics in the past, not generics about the future**
3. **Talk less, listen more**
4. **Take notes on emotions, not just facts**
5. **Follow the energy** - when they lean in, dig deeper

## 1.2 Workshops & Facilitation

### Discovery Workshop
**Duration:** 2-4 hours
**Participants:** 5-12 stakeholders

**Agenda:**
```
1. CONTEXT SETTING (15 min)
   - Project background
   - Workshop goals
   - Ground rules

2. PROBLEM EXPLORATION (45 min)
   - Individual: Write problems on sticky notes (5 min)
   - Share: Each person presents (15 min)
   - Cluster: Group similar problems (10 min)
   - Vote: Dot voting on priority (5 min)
   - Discuss: Top 3 problems (10 min)

3. USER JOURNEY MAPPING (60 min)
   - Define persona
   - Map current journey
   - Identify pain points
   - Envision future state

4. SOLUTION BRAINSTORM (45 min)
   - Silent brainstorm
   - Share and cluster
   - Feasibility/Impact matrix
   - Select top solutions

5. NEXT STEPS (15 min)
   - Action items
   - Timeline
   - Follow-up
```

### Requirements Prioritization Workshop
**Duration:** 2 hours
**Output:** Prioritized backlog

**Methods to use:**
- MoSCoW voting
- Buy-a-Feature (stakeholders have limited budget)
- 100-point method (distribute 100 points across features)
- Forced ranking

### Facilitation Best Practices
| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| Use timeboxes strictly | Let discussions run over |
| Ensure all voices heard | Let loudest dominate |
| Capture everything visually | Rely on memory |
| Park off-topic items | Dismiss tangents rudely |
| Summarize frequently | Assume shared understanding |

## 1.3 Observation & Contextual Inquiry

### Contextual Inquiry Method
Observe users in their natural environment while they work.

**Process:**
1. **Prepare**
   - Define what you want to learn
   - Schedule 2-hour sessions
   - Prepare observation guide

2. **Observe**
   - Watch silently first
   - Take detailed notes
   - Sketch the environment
   - Note workarounds

3. **Inquire**
   - "I noticed you did X, why?"
   - "What would happen if you didn't do that?"
   - "Is that typical?"

4. **Synthesize**
   - Affinity diagram findings
   - Identify patterns
   - Document insights

## 1.4 Surveys & Questionnaires

### When to Use Surveys
- Large user base (100+)
- Quantitative validation needed
- Geographic distribution
- Before/after measurement

### Survey Design Principles

**Question Types:**
| Type | When to Use | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Multiple choice | Discrete options | "Which feature do you use most?" |
| Likert scale | Measure attitude | "Rate your satisfaction 1-5" |
| Ranking | Prioritization | "Rank these features 1-5" |
| Open-ended | Discovery | "What would you change?" |
| Matrix | Multiple items, same scale | Feature satisfaction grid |

**Best Practices:**
1. Keep under 5 minutes (10-15 questions max)
2. Start easy, end with demographics
3. Avoid leading questions
4. Test with 5 people first
5. Offer incentive for completion

### Net Promoter Score (NPS)
```
"On a scale of 0-10, how likely are you to recommend [product] to a colleague?"

Promoters (9-10): Loyal enthusiasts
Passives (7-8): Satisfied but unenthusiastic
Detractors (0-6): Unhappy customers

NPS = % Promoters - % Detractors
```

### System Usability Scale (SUS)
10-question standardized usability questionnaire. Score benchmarks:
- 68+ = Above average
- 80+ = Good
- 90+ = Excellent

## 1.6 Prototyping for Requirements

### Prototype Fidelity Levels

| Level | Tools | Purpose | When |
|-------|-------|---------|------|
| **Sketch** | Paper, whiteboard | Quick ideation | Discovery |
| **Wireframe** | Figma, Balsamiq | Layout validation | Early design |
| **Mockup** | Figma, Sketch | Visual validation | Design phase |
| **Interactive** | Figma prototype | Flow validation | Pre-development |
| **Coded** | HTML/React | Technical validation | Complex interactions |

### Using Prototypes for Elicitation
1. Show, don't tell - reactions are more honest
2. Test with 5 users to find 80% of issues
3. Ask "what would you expect to happen?"
4. Note confusion points
5. Iterate based on feedback

## 3.1 MoSCoW Method

| Priority | Meaning | Guideline |
|----------|---------|-----------|
| **Must Have** | Non-negotiable for launch | ~60% of effort |
| **Should Have** | Important but not critical | ~20% of effort |
| **Could Have** | Desirable if time permits | ~20% of effort |
| **Won't Have** | Explicitly out of scope | Documented for future |

### When to Use
- Fixed timeframe projects
- Stakeholder alignment
- Scope negotiations

### Facilitation Tips
1. Start with "Won't Have" to set boundaries
2. Be strict about "Must Have" - life or death only
3. Document rationale for each placement
4. Review with stakeholders before finalizing

## 3.2 RICE Scoring

**Formula:**
```
RICE Score = (Reach Ã— Impact Ã— Confidence) / Effort
```

| Factor | Definition | Scale |
|--------|------------|-------|
| **Reach** | Users affected per quarter | Absolute number |
| **Impact** | Effect on each user | 0.25 (minimal) to 3 (massive) |
| **Confidence** | How sure are we? | 50% (low) to 100% (high) |
| **Effort** | Person-months required | Absolute number |

### Example
```
Feature: In-app notifications

Reach: 5000 users/quarter
Impact: 2 (high - improves retention)
Confidence: 80%
Effort: 2 person-months

RICE = (5000 Ã— 2 Ã— 0.80) / 2 = 4000
```

### RICE Scoring Template
| Feature | Reach | Impact | Confidence | Effort | Score |

## 3.7 Cost of Delay

Quantify the cost of NOT doing something:

```
Cost of Delay = Value/week Ã— Weeks delayed
```

**CD3 (Cost of Delay Divided by Duration):**
```
CD3 = Cost of Delay / Duration
```

Higher CD3 = Do first

### Types of Cost of Delay
1. **User/Business Value** - Revenue, users, satisfaction
2. **Time Criticality** - Deadline-driven (regulatory, seasonal)
3. **Risk Reduction** - Technical debt, security vulnerabilities

## 4.3 User Interview Best Practices

### The Mom Test Rules (Rob Fitzpatrick)
1. **Talk about their life, not your idea**
2. **Ask about specifics in the past, not generics about the future**
3. **Talk less, listen more**
4. **Bad data from compliments**
5. **Ideas and feature requests should be understood, not obeyed**

### Interview Script Template
```
OPENING (5 min)
"Thanks for meeting. I'm learning about [topic]. There are no right
answers - I just want to understand your experience."

CURRENT BEHAVIOR (15 min)
"Walk me through the last time you [did the thing]..."
"What happened next?"
"Why did you do it that way?"
"How often does this happen?"

PROBLEMS & WORKAROUNDS (10 min)
"What's the hardest part about [problem space]?"
"What have you tried to solve this?"
"Why didn't that work?"

MOTIVATION (5 min)
"What would it mean if you could solve this?"
"What's stopping you from solving it today?"

WRAP UP (5 min)
"Who else should I talk to?"
"Is there anything I should have asked?"
```

# SECTION 8: CASE STUDIES (NEW)

## Case Study 1: Successful Discovery - Saudi Fintech App

**Context:**
A Saudi bank wanted to launch a personal finance management app for young professionals.

**Approach:**
1. Conducted 20 user interviews across Riyadh, Jeddah, Dammam
2. Ran 3 discovery workshops with bank stakeholders
3. Created 4 personas based on research
4. Used Jobs-to-be-Done framework

**Key Findings:**
- Users wanted Shari'ah compliance visibility
- Salary timing created monthly cash flow anxiety
- Social spending (weddings, gifts) was unpredictable
- Users preferred Arabic interface

**Outcome:**
- 40% faster requirements sign-off
- MVP launched in 4 months
- 50,000 downloads in first month

**Lessons:**
1. Cultural context matters - Shari'ah compliance wasn't in original brief
2. Local holidays and events drive unique requirements
3. Face-to-face interviews yielded richer insights than surveys

## Case Study 2: Failed Requirements - E-commerce Platform

**Context:**
International e-commerce company expanding to Saudi Arabia.

**What Went Wrong:**
1. Assumed global requirements applied
2. No local user research
3. Ignored cultural preferences
4. Missed regulatory requirements

**Specific Failures:**
- No Arabic support at launch
- No mada payment option
- No Shari'ah-compliant product filtering
- Violated Saudi e-commerce regulations
- Delivery assumptions wrong (no house numbers)

**Impact:**
- 6-month delay for remediation
- SAR 2M additional development cost
- Negative brand perception at launch

**Lessons:**
1. Always validate requirements locally
2. Regulatory research must be early
3. "Lift and shift" doesn't work
4. Local payment methods are non-negotiable
